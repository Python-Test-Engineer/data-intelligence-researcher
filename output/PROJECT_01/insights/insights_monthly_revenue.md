## Key Observation

Revenue peaked sharply in March 2025 at approximately $25,000 before falling steeply through April and into May (~$14,000). Critically, order count did not track revenue: April had the highest order count (~12 orders) yet produced lower revenue than March (~11 orders), and May's revenue collapse corresponds to only ~6 orders. This divergence between volume and revenue points to declining average order value in the later months.

## Business / Scientific Implication

The March peak followed by two consecutive months of decline creates an early warning signal. If May's downturn is confirmed as a full-month pattern (not just partial data), the business faces a Q2 revenue trajectory problem. The April volume-revenue disconnect specifically suggests the sales team may be closing more deals but at smaller ticket sizes or higher discounts -- management should examine whether deal quality is eroding as the team chases volume targets.

## Deeper Analysis

The Feb-to-Mar revenue jump (~$17,000 to ~$25,000) is the single largest MoM movement at roughly +46%, yet order count only increased from ~9 to ~11 -- meaning the surge was driven by higher-value deals, not volume. Conversely, April added the most orders of any month (~12) but revenue fell ~9%, indicating a shift toward smaller or more heavily discounted transactions. May shows both volume and revenue collapsing simultaneously -- the worst of both dimensions. A casual reader might blame May's shortfall on "incomplete data," but even adjusting for partial-month, the AOV has clearly dropped from March's ~$2,270 to May's ~$2,378 (higher AOV but far fewer deals), suggesting pipeline issues rather than deal-quality issues in May specifically. The bar heights reveal a seasonal pattern: Q1 activity builds, peaks in Mar-Apr, then drops in May -- consistent with B2B budget cycles.

## Confidence Assessment

**Confidence:** Medium
**Rationale:** Clear trend visible in validated data, but only 5 months limits seasonality conclusions; May is likely a partial month which confounds interpretation.

## Suggested Next Steps

1. Confirm whether May data is complete or partial -- if partial, normalise May revenue to a full-month estimate before drawing trend conclusions.
2. Decompose the March peak by product/region/rep to identify what drove the outsized performance and whether it is repeatable.
