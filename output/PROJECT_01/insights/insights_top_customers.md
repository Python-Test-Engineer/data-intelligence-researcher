The immediate priority should be building a customer success / retention function to convert one-time buyers into repeat accounts.

This near-uniformity combined with 1-order-per-customer means the customer base is essentially undifferentiated: no "whale" accounts, no long-tail distribution, no Pareto concentration. The top 3 customers (Delta Corp, Ironclad Systems, Delphi Data) at $11,960 represent just 12.2% of total revenue -- far below the typical B2B 80/20 pattern. While this diversification is low-risk from a concentration standpoint, it is also low-opportunity from an account growth standpoint. Several customers cluster at exactly $3,000 (Mu Analytics, Yield Analytics) and $3,060 (Titan Web Group, Paragon Software), suggesting they purchased the same product at the same price point. This homogeneity in deal size indicates either a very narrow product range or a dominant price tier.
